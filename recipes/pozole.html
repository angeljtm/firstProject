Pozole Rojo (Mexican Pork and Hominy Stew)

Delicous pozole for you and the family!

Prepare yourself for a taste of Mexican culinary tradition with a homemade pozole recipe that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico City. Pozole, a rich and comforting stew, is a celebration of flavor and culture, and with this recipe, you can recreate that magic in your very own kitchen. So, gather your loved ones, set the table with warm tortillas and an assortment of toppings, and share in the joy of homemade pozole. This recipe invites you to embrace the warmth, the depth, and the soul-nourishing qualities of this beloved Mexican dish. Get ready to create memories and savor the magic that comes from a bowl of homemade pozole. ¡Buen provecho!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1 pound boneless pork loin, cubed
  • ½ pound pork neck bones
  • water to cover
  • 1 head garlic, cloves peeled
  • salt to taste
  • 1 large plum tomato
  • 4 ounces dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 clove garlic
  • ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 pinch ground cumin
  • 2 cups wate
  • 2 (16 ounce) cans white hominy, drained

Toppings:

  • shredded lettuce or cabbage
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 4 limes, quartered

Directions

  1. Place pork shoulder, pork loin, and pork neck bones in a large pot; cover with water. Add 1 head of garlic and salt to taste. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer until meat is tender and cooked through, about 1 hour. Stir in hominy; bring to a boil and simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, place tomato and guajillo chiles in a pot and add enough water to cover; bring to a boil. Cook until chiles have softened, about 15 minutes; drain.
  3. Place tomato and chiles with salt, 1 clove garlic, oregano, and cumin in a blender; add 2 cups water. Blend until smooth. Strain mixture through a fine-mesh sieve and set chile sauce aside.
  4. Transfer pork to a work surface and shred with 2 forks. Discard the pork bones and garlic.
  5. Stir chile sauce and shredded pork into the pot. Simmer pozole until flavors have blended, about 5 minutes.
  6. Ladle pozole into serving bowls and top with shredded cabbage and onion; serve lime wedge on the side.

Enjoy!
